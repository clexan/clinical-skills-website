This review consolidates the core habits from Part 1: stay structured during first assessment, interpret ECGs systematically, and switch quickly to ALS priorities when arrest is present.

## First assessment and context

- Always use the ABCDE approach for the initial assessment of an acutely ill patient. Do not skip steps. The goals of the assessment are a patent airway, adequate oxygenation and ventilation, haemodynamic stability, recognition and correction of neurological derangement when possible, and identification of likely causes.
- Include a SAMPLE history whenever possible. If the patient cannot provide it, ask family members, caregivers, or witnesses.

## ECG essentials

- In medical emergencies, use a structured 6-question approach for ECG rhythm analysis.
- Basic interpretation of a 12-lead ECG includes rhythm, rate, electrical axis, and ST-segment and T-wave changes.

## ALS priorities in cardiac arrest

- During cardiac arrest, high-quality CPR is fundamental. Without effective chest compressions and appropriate rescue breaths, other interventions have limited value.
- Follow the ALS algorithm. Identify the rhythm as early as possible and treat accordingly. High-quality CPR is equally important in both shockable and non-shockable rhythms because it maintains cerebral and coronary perfusion.
- In shockable rhythms, defibrillate immediately. Be familiar with the defibrillator and the energy settings used in your clinical environment before approaching a patient. Adrenaline and amiodarone are added in appropriate doses after the third defibrillation.
- In non-shockable rhythms, give adrenaline 1 mg IV as a rapid bolus.
- Reassess the rhythm every 2 minutes. After the first dose, repeat adrenaline every 3-5 minutes.
- Secure the airway, usually with an i-gel or another appropriate airway device, and establish IV or IO access after the first rhythm recognition. These steps must not delay rhythm recognition and initial treatment.
- Actively search for and treat reversible causes using the 4H and 4T framework.
- After return of spontaneous circulation, reassess the patient using ABCDE again. Normalize each parameter as much as possible and continue targeted post-resuscitation care.
