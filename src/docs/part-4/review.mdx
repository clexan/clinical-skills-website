## Review

In every patient with sudden neurological deterioration, begin with the **ABCDE approach** and assess the **Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)** when appropriate.

Not every cause of sudden neurological deterioration is primarily neurological. Systemic problems such as **hypoxia** and **hypotension** may produce major neurological impairment and must always be considered early.

If there are signs of **raised intracranial pressure (ICP)**, immediate supportive measures are required. Administer **mannitol**, elevate the head to **30°**, and, if appropriately trained, **sedate the patient**. **Short-term hyperventilation** may also be considered as a temporary measure.

In the case of **seizures**, first secure the **airway** and stop the seizure as soon as possible, for example with a **benzodiazepine** given by the easiest available route. At the same time, rapidly search for and treat the underlying cause, such as **hypoglycaemia**.

When **poisoning** is suspected, perform **basic decontamination**, administer **specific antidotes** when available, and consider contacting a **toxicology or poison control centre** for further guidance. Throughout management, maintain **supportive care of vital functions** until the toxic state resolves.
