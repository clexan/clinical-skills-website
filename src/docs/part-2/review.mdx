This review brings together the bedside decisions that matter most in Part 2: recognise acute coronary syndrome early, separate STE-ACS from NSTE-ACS, and escalate arrhythmia treatment according to haemodynamic risk.

## Start with ECG and risk recognition

- Start with the **ABCDE approach** in every patient.
- When treating **cardiac arrhythmias**, first look for life-threatening features: **shock, syncope, myocardial ischaemia, or severe heart failure**.

## Acute coronary syndrome priorities

- Acute coronary syndrome does not always present with the same symptoms, so do not rely only on a classic chest-pain pattern. Record and interpret the **12-lead ECG** carefully. The first major distinction is between **ST-elevation ACS (STE-ACS)** and **non-ST-elevation ACS (NSTE-ACS)** because treatment differs.
- For **all ACS patients**, give **acetylsalicylic acid 300 mg orally**. Treat pain with **morphine** if needed, keeping in mind that nausea may occur. Give **sublingual nitroglycerine** unless contraindicated. Administer **oxygen only if SpO₂ is below 90%**.
- In **STE-ACS**, if symptom onset is within **12 hours**, proceed to **primary PCI as soon as possible**. Add an oral **P2Y12 inhibitor** such as **prasugrel** or **ticagrelor**.
- In **NSTE-ACS**, management depends on **risk assessment**. Do **not** administer **P2Y12 inhibitors before coronary angiography**.

## Arrhythmia escalation

- A **tachycardia** with any life-threatening feature should be treated with **synchronised cardioversion** using the appropriate energy, with up to **three attempts**, followed by **amiodarone 300 mg IV over 10–20 minutes**.
- A **bradycardia** with life-threatening features should be treated with **atropine 0.5 mg IV**, repeated up to a total dose of **3 mg**. If there is no adequate response, proceed to **transcutaneous pacing**. Alternative drugs may be used, but pacing is the preferred escalation.
- Even without life-threatening features, some bradycardias still require treatment. Assess for **risk of asystole**, especially in the presence of **recent asystole, Mobitz II AV block, complete heart block with broad QRS complexes, or ventricular pauses longer than 3 seconds**.
