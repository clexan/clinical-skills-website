## Stick to the ABCDEs

## Keynotes

To diagnose sepsis, a patient should have a documented or suspected infection together with an increase in SOFA score of at least 2 points. Because SOFA is relatively time-consuming and impractical in acute settings, qSOFA is commonly used as the first bedside screening step.

### qSOFA

- Respiratory rate ≥ 22/min
- Systolic blood pressure < 100 mmHg
- Altered mental status with GCS < 15

## If Sepsis Is Suspected

- Obtain cultures.
- Administer broad-spectrum intravenous antibiotics.
- Search for signs of shock, especially hypotension and elevated lactate.
- Administer fluids at 30 mL/kg.
- Apply vasopressors if the patient remains hypotensive after 30 mL/kg of fluid resuscitation.

All of this should be initiated within one hour of suspected sepsis.
